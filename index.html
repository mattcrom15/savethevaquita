The Vaquita (Phocoena sinus) are one of the World's smallest, and now most endangered cetacean. It's name translates to 'little cow' in Spainsh. Their diet consists of fish and squid, and sometimes crab and lobster. In 1996 there had been reported to be around 600 Vaquitas in the wild. In 2015 it had dropped to 59 and now in 2020, it's down to around 10 remaining Vaquitas.

Location

Sea of Cortez

Northern Gulf of California

Mexico

Vaquita's only Habitat is in the Northern Gulf of California. They are not found anywhere else on earth. They live in shallow waters and spend most of their time underwater. However, Living in shallow waters puts them in the reach of illegal fishing nets.

Threats

The Vaquita's Habitat is threatened by illegal fishing boats, hunting for Totoaba. The Totoaba is hunted for mainly for it's bladder, of which is used in Chinese medicine and as a health food. Because both the Vaquita and the Totoaba occupy the same Habitat, they are both at risk of being caught/entangled in gill nets. These nets are designed to catch anything and everything, killing the Vaquita.
